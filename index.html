JOURNEY OF VIRAT KOHLI

  • 1988 Virat Kohli was born on 5th November in Delhi. He was raised in Uttam Nagar and started his schooling at Vishal Bharti Public School.
  • 1998 The West Delhi Cricket Academy was created, and Kohli, a nine-year-old, was part of its first intake. He trained at the academy under Rajkumar Sharma and also played matches at the Sumeet Dogra Academy at Vasundhara Enclave at the same time.
  • 2000 Fastest captain to score 2000 ODI runs. First Indian captain to score a hundred and a duck in the same Test. First captain to score 10 international hundreds in a calendar year. Most Test centuries as a captain of India (12). Most double centuries by a captain in Test cricket (6).
  • 2002Kohli first played for Delhi Under-15 team in October 2002 in the 2002–03 Polly Umrigar Trophy. He was the leading run-getter for his team in that tournament with 172 runs at an average of 34.40.
  • 2003 Virat became the captain of the team for the 2003–04 Polly Umrigar Trophy and scored 390 runs in 5 innings at an average of 78 including two centuries and two fifties.
  • 2004 In late 2004, he was selected in the Delhi Under-17 team for the 2003–04 Vijay Merchant Trophy. He scored 470 runs in four matches at an average of 117.50 with two hundreds and top-score of 251.
  • 2005 Delhi Under-17s won the 2004–05 Vijay Merchant Trophy in which Kohli finished as the highest run-scorer with 757 runs from 7 matches at an average of 84.11 with two centuries.
  • 2006 In July 2006, Kohli was selected in the India Under-19 squad on its tour of England. He averaged 105 in the three-match ODI series against England Under-19s and 49 in the three-match Test series. India Under-19 went on to win both the series.
  • 2008 In February–March 2008, Kohli captained the victorious Indian team at the 2008 ICC Under-19 Cricket World Cup held in Malaysia.
  • 2011 Kohli played in 2001 Cricket World Cup and India went on to win the match by six wickets and lift the World Cup for the first time since 1983.
  • 2012 Kohli was appointed the vice-captain for the 2012 Asia Cup in Bangladesh. Kohli was awarded the man of the match in both matches that India won.
  • 2013 In March 2013, Kohli started a charity foundation called Virat Kohli Foundation (VKF). The organisation aims at helping underprivileged kids and conducts events to raise funds for the charity.
